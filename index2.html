About

Yosh Halberstam is a political economist who leverages economic theory, big data, and econometric techniques to study polarization in politics and to shed light on the structural disadvantages of out-groups in the workplace.

Halberstam has published research leveraging Twitter data on the role of homophily and group size in ideological segregation and exposure to political information. His current work using social media data focuses on the effects of election outcomes on information polarization in the electorate. In other published research, he has studied the role of asymmetric information in US presidential elections and variation in judicial panel sizes on US state supreme courts.

Halberstam's current work examines the effect of pay transparency laws on pay gaps between worker groups. In a separate set of projects, he analyzes voice data from US Supreme Court litigators and lawyers at top-100 US law firms to study the role of norms and use of adaptive behaviours by out-groups in the workplace.
