About

I leverage economic theory, big data, and econometric techniques to shed light on structural disadvantages of out-groups in the workplace and study media and polarization in politics.

My current work uses voice samples to study the role of norms and use of adaptive behaviours by out-groups in the workplace. Another set of projects using administrative data examines the effect of pay transparency laws and unionization on pay gaps between gender groups and wage inequality in Canadian universities.

A separate research program using Twitter and US election data focuses on the effects of election outcomes on information polarization in the electorate and subsequent election outcomes. A second project examines the effect of local news on polarization in national elections.
